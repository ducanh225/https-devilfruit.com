Home > Zoan > Mogu Mogu no Mi

Statistics

Japanese Name: モグモグの実

Official English Name: Mole-Mole Fruit (Funimation); Diggy-Diggy Fruit (Viz, 4Kids)

Meaning: From Mogura (土竜), meaning Mole

Usage Debut: Chapter 184; Episode 113

Type: Zoan

Current User: Drophy

Etymology

"Mogu" is short for "Mogura" (土竜), the Japanese word for mole. In the VIZ Manga and 4Kids dub, it is called the Diggy-Diggy Fruit, a reference to the animal's ability to dig through earth. In the Funimation adaptations, it is correctly called the Mole-Mole Fruit.

Strengths and Weaknesses

The major strength of the fruit, as demonstrated by Drophy, is that the user can move underground as if swimming in a pool. They can also attack enemies with sharp mole claws the size of bananas. A powerful enough user can smash through stone structures without losing speed while traveling underground.

The tunnels that the user makes by digging can also be used by their allies to aid them in fighting. Unfortunately, the tunnels can also be used by enemies. A major weakness is that all the tunnels dug are interconnected, meaning a large-scale attack, like an explosion, sent into the tunnels could greatly damage the user and their allies. Other than that, the user is also affected by the same main weaknesses as other Devil Fruit users.

Usage

The fruit's powers, as used by Drophy, have been primarily for combat. During her battle against Usopp and Chopper, her mole hybrid form resembled the game of "whack-a-mole," especially when Usopp tried to use the 5-Ton Hammer on her while she kept retreating into her holes. The majority of her named techniques while in her mole-hybrid form were first seen during the same battle.

Techniques

Mogura Tonpo: Mogugyo (土竜遁法 土竜魚, Mogura Tonpō: Mogugyo, literally meaning "Mole Digging Technique: Mole-Fish"): Drophy leaps into the air and swan dives underground from the opening of one of her molehills. This technique is called Mole Swimming Stealth Style in the VIZ manga, Mole Swimming: Mole-Fish Escape in the Funimation dub, and Woodchuck Swimming Stealth Style in the 4Kids dub.

Mogura Banana (土竜 平手撃ちバナナ, Mogura Banana, literally meaning "Mole Palm Shoot"): Drophy attacks her opponent with her mole-hybrid form's claws, which can be performed while underground or by leaping out at her opponent. Its name is derived from the way she attacks and her claws resembling bananas. This is called Mole Banana in the VIZ manga and Funimation adaptations and Woodchuck Banana in the 4Kids dub.

Mogurazuka Highway (モグラ塚ハイウェイ, Mogurazuka Haiuei, literally meaning "Molehill Highway"): Drophy grabs her opponent's feet from underground and starts tunneling rapidly, dragging them along the surface. This is called Moletown Highway in the Viz Manga, Woodchuck Highway: Off and Running in the 4Kids dub, and Molehill Highway in the Funimation adaptations.

Mogurazuka Yonban Kousaten (モグラ塚4番交差点, Mogurazuka Yonban Kōsaten, literally meaning "Molehill Intersection Number 4"): A combo attack with Babe, where Drophy uses Mogurazuka Highway to carry the opponent past Babe, who then strikes the captive with his 4-ton bat, causing massive damage. This technique became her downfall when Usopp shouted it aloud while Drophy was in the pathway of Babe's bat, causing him to strike her by accident. This is called Moletown Cleanup Hitter in the Viz Manga and the Funimation dub of Movie 8, Mole Hill Cleanup Hitter in the Funimation subs of Movie 8, and Woodchuck Batter Blaster in the 4Kids dub.

Mogu Mogu Impact (モグモグ玉砕インパクト, Mogu Mogu Inpakuto, literally meaning "Mole Mole Honorable Death"): After performing Mogurazuka Highway, Drophy steers her opponent into a wall, smashing them into it with great force. This is simply called Mole Impact in the Viz Manga, Mole-Mole Impact in the Funimation adaptations, and Woodchuck Wallbanger in the 4Kids dub.

Translation and Dub Issues

In the 4Kids dub, Drophy's Baroque Works alias Miss Merry Christmas was changed to Miss Groundhog's Day, which also altered the animal into which the fruit allows her to turn from a mole to a groundhog. This change was only dialogue-wise, as she still looked like a mole. The name "Diggy Diggy" did not match the naming scheme of other Zoans as it did not list the species name.

Trivia

A running gag due to Drophy's shape when she turns into a mole hybrid is that Usopp mistakes her for a penguin. While the name of the fruit can be short for the Japanese word for mole, it can also abbreviate "mogu mogu," a phrase meaning to chew one's words, which describes Drophy's manner of speech.
