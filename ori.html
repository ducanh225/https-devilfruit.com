Home > Paramecia > Ori Ori no Mi

Statistics

Japanese Name: オリオリの実

Official English Name: Bind-Bind Fruit (Viz); Cage-Cage Fruit (4Kids, Funimation)

Meaning: Cage

Usage Debut: Chapter 217; Episode 130

Type: Paramecia

Current User: Hina

Description

The Ori Ori no Mi is a Paramecia-type Devil Fruit that allows the user to bind their foes in iron shackles created by the user's body, turning the user into a Driving Human (調教人間, Chōkyō Ningen, English: "Discipline-Woman"). It was eaten by Hina.

Etymology

Nori (乗り) means "to ride" or "to stitch" in Japanese. In the VIZ manga, it is called the Bind-Bind Fruit. It is called the Cage-Cage Fruit in the 4Kids and Funimation dubs.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

The major strength of the fruit is that it gives the user the ability to bind foes via iron-like constructs manifested from their body. So far, no character has been shown escaping those cuffs, so it is unknown if it is possible to break them away.

When the user makes contact with something they intend to bind, their body will bend in an unnatural manner and transform into something akin to a giant, restraining metal cuff around the spot contact was made (with the bending body part fully regenerating afterwards). It is also possible for the user to shackle more than one object at once, allowing them to secure a target to the nearby environment.

Weaknesses

The fruit does not seem to have any specific weaknesses outside the standard Devil Fruit weaknesses. However, the user can unintentionally bind allies and prevent them from fighting when utilizing more sophisticated, long-ranged techniques.

Usage

Hina has used the fruit's bestowed powers mostly for the subduing of opponents, usually through her own arms and legs. She can simply bind an opponent that passes through her, or employ something as complex as forming a cage from her arms to surround them. Hina's mastery with this fruit is what has earned her the epithet of Black Cage Hina.

Techniques

Awase Baori: After surrounding an area with a fence-like cage of bars that come out of her arms, Hina closes the cage and binds anyone that is caught inside. This was first seen being used against Luffy at Marineford. In the Viz Manga and Funimation adaptations, this is called Kimono Sleeve Cage.

Trivia

This Devil Fruit is thematically similar to the Kibi Kibi no Mi, the Muchi Muchi no Mi, and the non-canon Peto Peto no Mi, as they are all based on making things obey the user, and it is also similar to the Jake Jake no Mi, as they both grant the ability of controlling someone by being physically on someone.

This is the first manga-based Devil Fruit to reuse a name from a non-canon fruit: the "Nori Nori no Mi" had previously appeared in the One Piece Premier Show 2021.

When the fruit's power was first used, Bluegrass said that the fruit turned her into a Driving Human. However, SBS Volume 110 instead explained that the fruit made her a Piloting Human (操縦人間, Sōjū Ningen).
