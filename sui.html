Statistics

Japanese Name: スイスイの実

Official English Name: Swim-Swim Fruit (Viz, 4Kids); Smooth-Smooth Fruit (Funimation)

Meaning: Smooth Swim

Usage Debut: Chapter 732; Episode 664

Type: Paramecia

Current User: Senor Pink

Description

The Sui Sui no Mi is a Paramecia-type Devil Fruit that allows the user to swim in the ground or walls, making the user a Free-Swimming Human (遊泳自由人間, Yūei Jiyū Ningen, Viz: Free Swimmer). It was eaten by Senor Pink. This fruit's abilities were duplicated in the Seraph S-Shark, via a transfusion of Green Blood.

Etymology

Suisui is the Japanese onomatopoeia for any kind of smooth and fluid movement. Sui (水) is also one possible reading for the kanji for "water," as well as being half of the Japanese word for "swimming" (水泳, suiei), a reference to this ability's typical action in bodies of water.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

This fruit's main strength is that it allows the user to swim in solid matter such as ground and walls as if they were water, even allowing the user to swim upwards, seemingly defying gravity. It also allows the user to dive into the ground to avoid being injured by a fall. The fruit can also allow the user to move discreetly, as they will be mostly covered by the ground, allowing them to infiltrate places more easily.

Notably, the distorted objects retain their physical properties such as weight and solidity in regard to other people than the user. Furthermore, after being swum through, the distorted matter "flows" back into place like a liquid would, leaving no trace of the distortion.

Weaknesses

The user will need to physically swim to move, as opposed to instantaneously moving across surfaces like the Ishi Ishi no Mi. As such, the fruit's ability is limited by the user's swimming speed and form. Additionally, Senor Pink has not been shown to be able to breathe while submerged, so he very likely needs to resurface often to breathe. Lastly, the user is affected by the standard Devil Fruit weaknesses and as such cannot swim in actual bodies of water.

Usage

Senor Pink has been seen using his power to swim in the ground and sneak up on his enemy from behind, and has been stated to use it to infiltrate enemy bases and commit assassinations. He can also swim upward of a building to gain height for increased momentum for a suplex move.

Techniques

NyanNyan Suplex: Baby Buster: Senor Pink swims in the ground while picking up speed from a good distance away, before grabbing his opponent and swimming with them, all the while picking up even more speed. He then swims up a building with his opponent until they reach the top, and using the momentum, performs a suplex before letting gravity do the work. This technique is called Meow Meow Suplex: Baby Buster in the Viz Manga and FUNimation adaptations.

S-SharkS-Shark

S-Shark utilizes the power to swim through solid surfaces and sneak up on his enemies from below, similar to Senor Pink's usage. However, S-Shark adds a twist by taking advantage of the liquified surfaces to apply Fish-Man Karate style, creating waves to crush his opponents. S-Shark often uses the power to avoid further attacks after taking damage by dropping into a surface before retaking the initiative with a surprise attack.

History

Senor Pink ate the Sui Sui no Mi at no older than the age of 30, as he was seen using its powers to swim in the ground then. Dr. Vegapunk managed to duplicate this power by synthesizing Green Blood with Senor Pink's Lineage Factor and applying it onto the Seraphim S-Shark, who also uses the ability to liquefy solid surfaces as a means to apply Fishman Karate.

Trivia

While the Sui Sui no Mi gives the user the ability to swim through solid surfaces, due to the standard Devil Fruit weakness, the eater of this fruit remains unable to swim in water. This means that the Sui Sui no Mi is the only Devil Fruit shown so far that lets the user maintain the ability to swim under particular conditions. According to Oda, Sanji would have this Devil Fruit if he were allowed one.

The Anime originally depicted the affected ground/walls as the same color as their surroundings, much like the Manga. S-Shark's initial appearance in Episode 1095 made the ground/walls appear no different from ordinary water when affected, but this was reverted back to the original depiction in Episode 1101.
