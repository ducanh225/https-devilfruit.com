Home > Paramecia > Pero Pero no Mi

Statistics

Japanese Name: ペロペロの実

Official English Name: Lick-Lick Fruit

Meaning: Sound of licking

Usage Debut: Chapter 834; Episode 795

Type: Paramecia

Current User: Charlotte Perospero

Description

The Pero Pero no Mi is a Paramecia-type Devil Fruit that allows the user to create and manipulate candy at will, making the user a Candy Human (キャンディ人間, Kyandi Ningen, VIZ: "Candyman"). It was eaten by Charlotte Perospero.

Etymology

Peropero (ぺろぺろ) is the Japanese onomatopoeia for licking. In the English sub, it is called the Lick-Lick Fruit, coming from the action one would use to eat candy.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

This fruit allows the user to create and control seemingly limitless amounts of candy, even being able to make it move as if it were a mechanical construct, like escalators and prostheses. The candy created by the user is edible.

When hardened, the candy becomes incredibly strong and resilient, able to hold down the superhuman children of the Vinsmoke Family and even an entire moving castle, as well as block cannon fire and a Gear 3 punch from Monkey D. Luffy. Water also does not seem to affect the candy, as Perospero can comfortably travel across the ocean by riding a mobile mass of it around.

Weaknesses

The candy conjured by the fruit's powers will melt after a certain time. Similarly, intense heat being applied with force can cause the candy to shatter or melt away. If the user dies or is severely injured enough, all the candy conjured will vanish. Other than that, the fruit is susceptible to the standard Devil Fruit weaknesses.

Usage

Perospero uses this ability to move through the ground undetected, as he arrived to confront the Peachbeard Pirates this way and infiltrated Mary Geoise by tunneling through the Red Line. His tunnels sometimes also help other Revolutionaries with infiltration by creating underground rooms in which they can hide. He also uses it in combat.

After losing his arm due to an explosion caused by Pedro, Perospero used his ability to replace his lost limb with a prosthetic made of candy. This candy arm seemingly does not distract Perospero from his normal pirate duties, although he did mention the concern of it melting while drinking hot tea.

Techniques

Candy Man: Perospero coats an opponent with candy, which will then slowly seep into their body. After three minutes, the candy will completely fill their insides and stop their breathing, killing them. It was first mentioned to threaten Caesar Clown in case he did not finish his Gigantification project.

Candy Escalator: Perospero waves his candy cane and conjures a massive spiraling candy escalator that works like a regular one. It was first used to carry the Underworld emperors to the rooftop of the Whole Cake Chateau.

Candy Wave: Perospero binds a target with candy, immobilizing them. It was first used on the Vinsmoke Family and later on Bege's Big Father form, but was only named when used on the Thousand Sunny to prevent the Sanji Retrieval Team from setting sail.

Candy Wall: Perospero creates a large wall of candy to block attacks. The wall is sturdy enough to block cannon fire from Capone Bege's Big Father, as well as a Gear 3rd-enhanced punch from Luffy. It was first used to protect Big Mom from Bege's attack.

Candy Maiden: Perospero creates a giant iron maiden out of candy and attempts to trap his opponents inside it. It was first used in an attempt to prevent the Sanji Retrieval Team from reaching the Thousand Sunny, but it was melted by the flames of Luffy's Gomu Gomu no Red Hawk before it could trap any of the Straw Hats.

Candy Armor: Perospero protects himself from harm with candy, presumably by covering himself with it like armor. This technique has not been shown in the manga, only being mentioned by Perospero after he survived Pedro's suicidal explosion, with him being surprised that it was not strong enough to protect him from the attack.

Ame Umiushi: Perospero creates a large blob of candy resembling a sea slug and uses it as a means of maritime transportation, moving it across the water and allowing people to walk on top of it. It was first used to carry Big Mom across the sea to chase the Thousand Sunny.

Candy Shower: After forming a bow and producing dozens of arrows made of candy, Perospero shoots several arrows skyward at once. The arrows eventually descend rapidly, piercing anything in their path. This technique is known as Apocalyptic Candy Shower in the Viz manga and Funimation adaptations.

Non-Canon Techniques

Candy Maiden Decoration: An anime-only technique where Perospero gathers the remains of his destroyed Candy Maiden and reshapes them into a larger construct resembling a giant mouth lined with spike-like teeth that he can fire at his foes.

Candy Wheel: Perospero creates a large, green candy wheel that spins beneath him, binding and damaging his opponents. He then molds his candy staff into a large hammer and jumps in the air, smashing it into his opponents.

Candy Sting: Perospero creates a puddle of candy underneath his opponents' feet, which then protrudes large, sharp candy spikes to stab them.

Trivia

Although it is non-canon, this fruit is very similar to Gasparde's Ame Ame no Mi, as both can manipulate and produce candy. The fruit bears similarities to Galdino's Doru Doru no Mi, as both allow the user to produce and manipulate a substance that can be hardened and act mechanically.

Both Devil Fruits are weak to fire and heat, and both users are high-ranking members of organizations antagonistic to the Straw Hat Pirates. Perospero's ability to replace a lost limb with a prosthesis made from his ability is similar to how Kuzan has replaced a destroyed leg with a replica made of ice.

In the anime, Perospero is able to turn his candy into many different colors.
