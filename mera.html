Statistics

  • Japanese Name: メラメラの実 (Mera Mera no Mi)
  • Official English Name: Flame-Flame Fruit
  • Meaning: Flame
  • Usage Debut: Chapter 489
  • Type: Logia
  • Current User: Sabo
  • Previous User: Portgas D. Ace
  • Fruit Category: Paramecia (in terms of elemental control)
  • Rarity: Rare

    • Etymology

    The name "Mera Mera" is derived from the Japanese onomatopoeia for flames, reflecting the fruit's ability to create and control fire. The English name "Flame-Flame Fruit" emphasizes its fiery nature, while the term "Flare-Flare Fruit" used in some adaptations highlights its explosive potential.

    Strengths and Weaknesses

    The Mera Mera no Mi grants its user the ability to create, control, and transform into fire, making them nearly invulnerable to most physical attacks. The user can produce devastating fire-based attacks, allowing for both offensive and defensive maneuvers. Additionally, the flames can be used to enhance physical attacks, increasing their destructive power.

    However, the fruit has notable weaknesses. It is inferior to the Magu Magu no Mi (Magma-Magma Fruit) in terms of elemental hierarchy, as magma is significantly hotter than fire. This means that attacks from a magma user can bypass the Mera Mera no Mi's intangibility. Furthermore, like all Devil Fruits, the user is vulnerable to seawater and Kairoseki (Sea-Prism Stone), which can nullify their powers.

    Usage

    Portgas D. Ace, the original user of the Mera Mera no Mi, utilized its powers in various ways, often combining them with his combat skills. He was known for his flamboyant fighting style and his ability to create massive firestorms. Ace's signature move, Hiken (Fire Fist), became iconic, showcasing the fruit's destructive capabilities.

    After Ace's death, Sabo inherited the fruit's powers and quickly adapted to them. Sabo's fighting style incorporates his own martial arts techniques, allowing him to use the Mera Mera no Mi in conjunction with his Ryusoken style. This combination enhances his combat effectiveness, making him a formidable opponent.

    Techniques

  • Hiken (Fire Fist): A powerful attack where the user ignites their fist and launches a column of fire at the target. This technique is capable of causing massive destruction.
  • Dai Enkai: Entei (Great Flame Commandment: Flame Emperor): Ace's ultimate technique, where he creates a gigantic fireball resembling the sun and hurls it at his opponent.
  • Moeru Ryusoken: Kaen Ryuo (Burning Dragon Claw Fist): A combination of Sabo's Ryusoken style and the Mera Mera no Mi, where he ignites his claws and delivers a powerful attack.
  • Higan (Fire Gun): The user shoots compressed fire bullets from their fingertips, allowing for rapid-fire attacks.
  • Enjomo (Blaze Net): A technique that creates a circle of fire around a designated area, trapping opponents inside.
  • Hidaruma (Fire Daruma): A technique that sends multiple fireballs towards the target, overwhelming them with flames.

    • Trivia

    The Mera Mera no Mi is notable for being the first Logia fruit not consumed by an antagonist, as both Ace and Sabo are allies of the Straw Hat Pirates. This fruit symbolizes the bond between the three brothers: Luffy, Ace, and Sabo.

    Interestingly, Ace's ultimate attack, which involved creating a small sun, foreshadowed the connection between Luffy's Gomu Gomu no Mi and the Sun God Nika, further emphasizing the theme of fire in their family lineage.

    Additionally, the Mera Mera no Mi is the second Devil Fruit shown to return to circulation after its user's death, following the Sara Sara no Mi, Model: Axolotl. This highlights the unique nature of Devil Fruits and their impact on the world of "One Piece."

    Throughout the series, the color of Ace's flames evolved from red-orange to a more yellow-orange hue, while Sabo's flames returned to the original red-orange color when he first used the fruit, showcasing the distinct styles of both characters.
