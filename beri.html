Statistics

Japanese Name: ベリベリの実

Official English Name: Berry-Berry Fruit

Meaning: Berry

Usage Debut: Chapter 426; Episode 309

Type: Paramecia

Current User: Very Good

Description

The Beri Beri no Mi is a Paramecia-type Devil Fruit that allows the user to transform their body parts into a multitude of berry-like balls, making the user a Grape Human (ブドウ人間, Budō Ningen). It was eaten by Very Good.

Etymology

"Beri" (ベリー) is the Japanese inflection of the English word "berry." It should not be confused with "Belly," the currency of the One Piece world. In the Viz Manga and Funimation dub, it is called the Berry-Berry Fruit.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

Upon eating the fruit, the user can divide themselves into several spherical, cannonball-like projectiles. This grants the user immunity to blunt attacks, similar to how the Bara Bara no Mi makes its user immune to cutting attacks. For example, an attack like Franky's punch, Strong Right, appears to be nullified.

In the anime, the user's body parts can float in midair like Buggy's and swarm around the opponent, allowing each ball to charge at the opponent from different angles. In the manga, the balls were only able to bounce. Unlike the Bara Bara no Mi, the Beri Beri no Mi's power allows the user to levitate all separate parts of their body, including the feet.

Weaknesses

Unfortunately, any defensive advantages granted by this ability come at the expense of strength and speed, as the user's charging attacks can be blocked by both Franky's arms and Zoro's swords. Unlike the Bara Bara no Mi, it seems that the user's body can only be split once, and the balls cannot be split further into smaller parts. The user also needs to keep track of all of its parts; if an opponent grabs one of the balls, there is no easy way to recover it. Franky defeated Very Good by grabbing his head, leaving him defenseless. Other than that, the user is also affected by standard Devil Fruit weaknesses.

Usage

The Beri Beri no Mi allows the user to transform into a group of balls, dubbed "berries." Once the user's body is divided, they can attack the opponent from multiple angles, as the balls can be launched at the opponent as projectiles.

Trivia

This fruit's power can be considered the polar opposite of the Bara Bara no Mi, as the latter is immune to blades but not blunt attacks, while the former is immune to blunt attacks but not blades.
