Meaning: Barrier

Type: Paramecia

Previous User: Kurozumi Semimaru

Current User: Bartolomeo

Description

The Bari Bari no Mi is a Paramecia-type Devil Fruit that allows the user to generate barriers, making them a Barrier Human (バリア人間, Baria Ningen). It was eaten by Kurozumi Semimaru, and upon its reincarnation, it was eaten by Bartolomeo.

Etymology

"Bari" comes from Baria (バリア), the Japanese pronunciation of the word "barrier." In the VIZ translation, Funimation subs, and the Funimation dub of Episode 970, it is called the Barrier-Barrier Fruit. In the Funimation dub, it is called the Bari-Bari Fruit.

Strengths

The fruit's power allows the user to conjure physical barriers out of thin air—either invisible or visibly transparent, resembling tinted panes or domes of glass. This is often achieved by the user making a finger-crossing gesture, though this does not appear to be required. Such barriers can shield the user against attacks, usually from the front, but also from other directions depending on the barrier's shape and position.

The barriers boast immense defensive power. Bartolomeo has a high opinion of his barriers' strength, claiming they are "unbreakable" and can stop Doflamingo's Birdcage. Powerful attacks that the Bari Bari no Mi's barriers have been able to completely block include:

  • Kouzuki Oden's sword slashes, which could bisect the gigantic Mountain God and damage Kaidou.
  • Elizabello II's King Punch, which can blow a massive hole into a fortress.
  • A punch capable of breaking 4000 tiles.
  • Dellinger's kicks, which can easily move ten tons of weight.
  • Hakuba's super-fast sword attacks.
  • Gladius' self-rupture.

The barrier can also be used offensively, such as by launching it forward to create powerful forces to strike targets or to deflect forces sent by enemies right back at them. Bartolomeo can form his barriers into solid constructs of various shapes, such as stairs, making this fruit useful for reaching hard-to-reach places.

Weaknesses

While it is unknown if the barriers have a breaking point, they still have several weaknesses:

  • The user can only sustain one barrier at a time.
  • There is a limit to how much barrier surface can be deployed at once.
  • Certain barrier shapes can still be outflanked by going around, above, or below them.
  • Bartolome o needs to move or reshape his barriers to attack, trading the barrier's protection for offense.
  • The fruit does not grant the user superhuman durability, making them vulnerable if the barrier is bypassed.
  • The barriers are not usually soundproof, as seen when Bartolomeo was unable to block certain taunts.

Additionally, it shares the standard weaknesses of Devil Fruits, including the inability to swim.

Usage

Kurozumi Semimaru

Semimaru, the former user of this fruit, used a barrier to protect Wano's then-new shogun Orochi from being killed by Kouzuki Oden. It is unknown if he ever used the fruit in any other instances.

Bartolomeo

Bartolomeo uses the fruit's power to protect himself in combat, often sitting idly while his foes injure themselves against his barriers. He can also use the shields offensively to strike down and repel targets or as walls to immobilize and crush victims. The barriers can serve supplementary purposes, such as forming stairways to reach higher areas.

Techniques

The named techniques that involve the Devil Fruit powers include:

  • Barrier: Bartolomeo activates his fruit's ability by crossing his fingers, creating a transparent barrier for protection.
  • Barrier Crash: Bartolomeo launches a barrier towards the enemy at high speed, capable of creating a shock wave.
  • Barrier Bulls: Bartolomeo charges forward with a curved barrier, knocking everything in his path.
  • Barrier Ball: A spherical barrier that prevents anything inside from escaping.
  • Barrierbility: Bartolomeo changes the shape of his barriers into various forms while retaining their resilience.
  • Stairs: He forms barriers into a flight of stairs, allowing movement in the air while providing protection.
  • Racket: A barrier shaped like a tennis racket, used to protect someone or something.
  • Homage Shinken: A technique where Bartolomeo forms a small barrier around his fist to enhance its power.
  • Bari Bari no Pistol: A punch amplified by a barrier, serving as a homage to Luffy's Gomu Gomu no Pistol.

History

The Bari Bari no Mi was eaten over 41 years ago by Kurozumi Semimaru. After his death, Bartolomeo found it after it had returned to circulation and consumed it.

Anime and Manga Differences

In the anime, Bartolomeo claims he cannot deploy barriers without crossing his fingers, which contradicts instances where he uses the technique without doing so. This inconsistency has been addressed in various ways throughout the series.

Trivia

The gesture Bartolomeo performs with his fingers when summoning his barrier is commonly known in Japan as a way to repel "bad things" or provoke others, making it a common taunt among school-age children and delinquents.
