Home > Paramecia > Suke Suke no Mi

Statistics

Japanese Name: スケスケの実

Official English Name: Clear-Clear Fruit

Meaning: See-through; Sheer

Usage Debut: Chapter 444; Episode 339 (Absalom), Chapter 925; Episode 917 (Shiryu)

Type: Paramecia

Previous User: Absalom

Current User: Shiryu

Description

The Suke Suke no Mi is a Paramecia-type Devil Fruit that gives its user the ability to turn themselves and whatever they touch (living or nonliving) invisible, making them a Clear Human (透明人間, Tōmei Ningen, VIZ: "Invisible Man"). It was initially eaten by Absalom, and after his death, it was transferred to Shiryu.

Etymology

Sukesuke (透け透け) is Japanese for "see-through" or "sheer," often used in regards to clothing. It comes from suku (透く), which means "to be transparent." In a nod to the perverseness of the fruit's initial user, its name may also play on sukebe (助け平), which means "lewdness/lewd person."

Strengths

The Suke Suke no Mi's primary ability is to make the user invisible, but the user can also selectively turn anything they are in contact with invisible as well. This allows the user to spy on conversations and people without being detected, as well as abduct people with no chance of being tracked. The user can even make an entire ship invisible for a concealed getaway.

Weaknesses

The user must be in close proximity to the target to extract their lifespan, and the target needs to feel fear towards the user. If the target is fearless, they become immune to soul extraction. Additionally, the user must invoke an incantation for the extraction to work, meaning that if the user is silenced, the targets will not be affected.

While the homies are typically subservient to the user, they possess their own minds and can turn against their creator if motivated enough. The user also experiences the standard Devil Fruit weaknesses to water and Seastone.

Usage

Absalom

Absalom uses the ability to turn invisible to aid him in combat; he claimed that his powers only serve as a supplementary to the modifications made to his body. He also uses his invisibility to peep on and sexually harass women as well as getting insight as a reporter.

Shiryu

Like Absalom, Shiryu also uses this fruit in combat. By turning himself and his sword invisible, he is able to cut opponents without being spotted, primarily for sneak attacks.

Techniques

Skating: Absalom turns his entire body invisible. This technique was first used against several of the Straw Hats on the Thousand Sunny, but was first named against Sanji.

Shisha no Te: Absalom aims one or both of his arms at an enemy and fires the bazookas on his arms; however, since the bazookas and their ammunition are invisible, the resulting attack appears to be some sort of concussive blast.

Kaijin no Te: While invisible, Absalom punches his opponent multiple times. This was first used against Sanji.

Kaijin no Ashi: While invisible, Absalom kicks his opponent multiple times. This was first used against Sanji.

History

At some point in the past, Absalom found the Suke Suke no Mi and ate it. Two years after the Summit War of Marineford, Absalom was abducted by the Blackbeard Pirates, who then killed him and used an unknown technique that allowed Shiryu to take the Suke Suke no Mi's powers from him before it could return to circulation.

Trivia

Sanji claimed that he wanted to eat this Devil Fruit because he wanted to be a superhero, although in reality, he wanted to peek at nude women while they were bathing. Despite being the only Devil Fruit that he was ever interested in, he lost interest after defeating Absalom, deciding to peek at women without its power.

This fruit is one of only three where its previous user was introduced after its current user, along with the Mane Mane no Mi and Bari Bari no Mi.
